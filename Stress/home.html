Stress

Stress is the body reaction to any change that requires an adjustment to everyday life. Stress causes the body to enact a fight or flight response. This response is when the body sympathetic nervous system realizes there is a possible threat, so it prepares itself for a situation where it should either fight or run.

Positive: When a good change occurs that requires adjustment or any change that causes a person to become more alert, aware, or excited.
Negative: When a person faces constant stress without relief nor relaxation.

This prolonged, negative stress is called distress. Distress occurs when a person is unable to cope with stress, causing them to lose homeostasis. Unfortunately, distress may cause people to change their behavior, attitude, or physiological control in a manner that may damage them later. When someone faces distress, they should seek help since they are no longer able to cope with their ordinary stress.


Stress Reducing Tips

  • Keep a positive attitude
  • Accept that there are events that you cannot control
  • Be assertive instead of aggressive
  • Eat healthy and be active
  • Make time for hobbies and interests
  • Get enough sleep
  • Do not rely on drugs or alcohol to combat stress
